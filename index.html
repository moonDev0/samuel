When to Go on an African Safari

Where to go on an African safari in January

  • This dry month is perfect for a Chimpanzee and/or Gorilla Trek as part of an African safari in Uganda or Rwanda.
  • The end of the rainy season is one of the best months to see Tanzanias migrating wildebeest, zebra, and gnu, particularly near Tanzania’s southern Ndutu and Salei plains.
  • The dry weather makes wildlife spotting much easier in the Maasai Mara which is a gorgeous safari in Kenya to go on.

Where to go on an African safari in February

  • This hot, wet summer month in South Africa is ideal to have a Big 5 Safari in Kruger National Park or have a relaxing holiday in the metropolitan beauty of Cape Town.
  • The warm, dry weather in Uganda and Rwanda provides ideal gorilla trekking conditions.
  • Visit Kenya, Tanzania, or Uganda to see newly born animals.

Where to go on an African safari in March

  • Visit the Serengeti for fantastic wildlife viewing and observe young baby wildebeests explore their new surroundings right in front of you.
  • The warm weather in South Africa makes this month ideal to go on a Kruger National Park or Sabi Sands safari.
  • Early in this month is a great time to climb Kilimanjaro as the weather is quite dry, relatively cool, and its very quiet.

Where to go on an African safari in April

  • This is a great time to explore the Okavango Delta rivers in a mokoro and do many Victoria Falls activities, such as river rafting.
  • Visiting Tanzania’s northern parks at this time is great as it is by far the wettest month of the year, and the cost of accommodation, such as the Ngorongoro Crater Lodge, is also heavily reduced during this season.

Where to go on an African safari in May

  • A great time to holiday in Mozambique as it is not yet peak season (less busy, and better value), but the weather is generally fine and warm (not too humid) and the AZGO Festival of arts and music brings an added flair to the capital.
  • The Fish River Canyon walk opens for the winter season at the beginning of the month. Experience great weather, lush greenery and blue skies while attempting this adrenaline-pumping hike.
  • Even though the rainy season is over, the Okavango Delta is steadily filling up with water from the Angolan highlands. The Okavango Delta’s rushing waterways bring in plenty of wildlife viewing opportunities during this period.

Where to go on an African safari in June

  • The peak season for great safari game viewing in Zimbabwe is from June through September.
  • It’s a busy time of year, but it’s also one of the greatest months to have a safari in Tanzania to experience the Great Migration.
  • An ideal time for beach holidays on the Indian Ocean coastline of the KwaZulu-Natal coast in South Africa, which tends to be temperate to hot over the nominal winter months.

Where to go on an African safari in July

  • The Great Migration (hundreds of thousands of wildebeest and zebra) enters the Maasai Mara from Tanzania in July to August, presenting one of the most magnificent wildlife spectacles on the planet.
  • An ideal time for combining a game viewing safari in Kenya with a beach holiday on the coast, being the coolest month, and relatively dry.
  • A holiday in Mozambique is fantastic with plenty of sunlight and very little wind. Diving and fishing are also excellent at this time of year.

Where to go on an African safari in August

  • Zimbabwe is likewise at the top of its game when it comes to safaris in August. After witnessing all the amazing wildlife, tourists can proceed to Victoria Falls for more adrenaline-pumping thrills.
  • An excellent time to have a safari in Zimbabwe, as days are sunny, daytime temperatures are mild (dropping down to below 10C at night), and wildlife viewing in Zimbabwe is picture-perfect.
  • The Serengeti National Park in Tanzania is a great attraction at this time of year, with the Great Migration taking centre stage, with daily river crossings in the Lamai/Kogatende region of the park.
  • The game viewing in the Etosha National Park is at its peak, making Namibia one of the finest safari destinations.

Where to go on an African safari in September

  • Tanzania is at its height during the Great Migration at this time, with continuous river crossings and elephants thundering past.
  • The Okavango, Moremi, Chobe and the Linyanti area in Botswana are absolutely world-class Wildlife watching opportunities at this time of year.
  • Mozambique is in the midst of its peak season, with temperatures rising throughout the day but remaining moderate at night. Due to the ending of most summer vacations, tourist numbers are lower, making this a perfect time to explore the country.

Where to go on an African safari in October

  • Because the bush in Zimbabwe is still relatively dry, animals cluster around the most popular water sources which make for great wildlife viewing.
  • This is one of the best periods for a game viewing safari in Namibia as it is the peak of the dry season making water-scarce, compelling animals to cluster around the remaining permanent waterholes.
  • An ideal time for a low-cost safari in Kenya (prices are lower than typical) for anyone that wants an adventure that will knock their hiking boots off. Also, the game is still active in Kenya.

Where to go on an African safari in November

  • A great month to have a holiday in Mauritius with temperatures having risen over the past few months but not yet too hot and humid.
  • This is a lovely time of year to vacation in Cape Town. Spring is turning into summer and means that people can take full advantage of everything this amazing city has to offer.
  • Kenya’s brief rains typically begin now and persist for a few weeks. However, this does not distract much from a fantastic wildlife encounter, and Kenya remains an excellent safari location.

Where to go on an African safari in December

  • See the home of the white lion while enjoying a safari in the Timbavati Game Reserve or go on an outstanding big game watching safari in the Kruger’s Park.
  • This is a fantastic month to visit Mauritius, with temperatures reaching their highest point of the year. The benefit of this month is that humidity levels are not as high as they are in January, making it ideal beach weather for sun lovers.
  • The summer rain means this is a great time for a bird watching safari in Zimbabwe.
